Glute Bridge

How to do Glute Bridge


  1. 1. Lie face down on the floor with your knees bent and your feet flat on the floor. Place your arms by your side with your palms facing down.
  2. 2. Lift your hips off the ground until your knees, hips, and shoulders are in a straight line. Strengthen your buttocks and pull your abs so that you do not strain your lower back during exercise.
  3. 3. Hold the bridge position for a few seconds before lowering the leg.

Bench Hip Thrust

Hip thrust increases gluteal strength and volume in ways that many other exercises cannot, and experts agree that it has benefits for many people, from athletes to seniors of age 65 and older.

Hip Thrust GIFs

How to Do Bench Hip Thrust Properly

  1. 1. With your knees bent and your feet flat on the floor, rest your back on a high surface (such as a bench or box).
  2. 2. The bench should touch your shoulder blades and your feet should be shoulder-width apart. You can rest your elbows on the bench.
  3. 3. With your chin pressed, press your heels until your thighs are parallel to the floor. The legs should be at a 90 degree angle.
  4. 4. Squeeze the upper gluteal muscles and then return to the beginning.

    5. If you are a beginner, aim for 3 sets of 12 rep and do your best up to 20 times by weight. Then proceed with exercise by experimenting with one-legged variations or by safely adding weight using either barbells, plates, or dumbbells.

Swiss Ball Hamstring Curl

Swiss Ball Hamstring Curl is a gymnastics and fitness ball exercise primarily aimed at hamstrings.

How to do Swiss Ball Hamstring Cur

  1. 1. Lie on your back with your back flat on the floor and your heels propped up on the Swiss Ball. For stability, keep your arms flat on the floor and in line with your shoulders.
  2. 2. Then, raise your hips so that they are in line with your knees and shoulders.
  3. 3. Begin the hamstring curl by rolling the ball backward toward your buttocks, with your feet flat on the ball and your knees extended toward the ceiling. Return to the starting position by reversing your movement. This completes one rep.
