Jumping Ropes

Jumping rope is a fun playground activity that also serves as a great way to get some exercise while impressing your friends. Whether you want to learn how to jump rope, learn some new tricks, or double-dutch, these simple steps will help you get there.

How to do Jump Ropes


  1. 1. Choose the appropriate rope. You should be able to find a rope that, when folded in half, reaches nearly up to your shoulders. This will give you enough space to jump over it comfortably without tripping over the rope.
  2. 2. Pick up the rope and stand up. Take each of the rope's handles in your hands. Extend your hands and forearms at a 45-degree angle, at least a foot away from your body. This will result in a wider arc for you to jump through. trip on the rope
  3. 3. Pass your foot over the rope. The rope should be hung behind you so that the middle of it touches the back of your feet.
  4. 4. Swing the rope over your head using your hands and wrists. Don't move your arms; instead, try to keep the motion to your wrists.
  5. 5. Hop over the rope as it approaches the front of your feet. Stand on your tiptoes and push off with your feet's balls. Maintain motion in your ankles. Bending your knees to jump will make the exercise much more difficult.
  6. 6. Set a pace that is comfortable for you. For beginners, it's best to move the rope slowly enough to allow for a small hop in between each jump over the rope. Maintain a straight back and a straight gaze as you settle into the proper rhythm. You'll be less likely to lose your balance if you keep an eye on your feet as you jump.

Single Leg Calf Raise

How to do Single Leg Calf

  1. 1. Place the ball of your right foot on a raised surface while holding a dumbbell by your side (the base of an incline bench or a weight plate works well).
  2. 2. Cross your left ankle behind your right, use your left hand to balance, and lower your right heel toward the floor (but do not touch it).
  3. 3. Lift the heel of your right foot as high as you can, squeezing your right calf at the top.
  4. 4. Pause for a moment before lowering yourself back to your starting position. Perform the same number of reps on both sides.

Standing calf raises

Stand on an exercise or yoga mat with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointing forward. If you don't have a mat, you can perform standing calf raises on the floor. Keep your back straight, shoulders back and down, and abs pulled in.

How to do Standing calf raises

  1. 1. Raise your heels gradually while keeping your knees extended (but not locked).
  2. 2. Pause for one second when you're standing on the tips of your toes as much as possible.
  3. 3. Return to the starting position by lowering your heels to the ground.
