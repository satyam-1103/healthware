Push Ups

Push-ups increase the strength of both the upper body and the core. 1 There are many changes. Beginners can start with the easy version, but advanced users can use the more difficult version. Push-ups can be done as part of weight exercise, circuit training, and strength training.

Push Up GIFfrom Push GIFs

How to do Push Ups


  1. 1. Pull your navel toward your spine to contract your abdominal muscles and tone your core.
  2. 2. Bend your elbows slowly and inhale, then lower them to the floor until your elbows are at a 90 degree angle.
  3. 3. As you exhale, contract your chest muscles, push them out with your arms, and return to the starting position.

Flat bench press

Equiment Required : barbell or dumbbells, flat bench

How to Do Flat Bench Press

  1. 1. Lie on your back on a bench with your knees bent and your feet flat on the floor. Grasp the barbell with your thumbs and palms facing your feet. Lift the weight off the rack by pressing your arms straight toward the ceiling.
  2. 2. Move the weight over chest level.
  3. 3. Bend your elbows at a 45-degree angle and slowly lower the weight to your chest. Keep the bar approximately in line with the nipple.
  4. 4. Pause, then return the weight to its original position.
  5. 5. Complete sets of 8-12 repetitions.

Chest Dip

Equipment required : Dip station.

Dips Chest Day GIFfrom Dips GIFs

How to do Chest Dips

  1. 1. Stand facing two parallel bars and hold them inside with your palms.
  2. 2. Straighten your elbows, press your palms down, and lift your body so that it is in line with your hands.
  3. 3. Then bend your elbows and lower your chest towards your arms.
  4. 4. Pause and then return to the starting position. Repeat
  5. 5. Do 3 sets of 8-12 repetitions.
